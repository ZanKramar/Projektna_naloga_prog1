  5. MikeB

    A great game, but like others have said it has its share of issues. To start with my gripes, my biggest complaint with this game is its A great game, but like others have said it has its share of issues. To start with my gripes, my biggest complaint with this game is its uneven AI - note how I said "uneven" as opposed to "bad". Because the AI isn't necessarily bad; in most instances it's actually quite good. Enemies work as teams, circling and flanking you in an attempt to take you out. They will stage coordinated assaults and work around offensive strengths on your part. At other times, the AI will fail. Miserably. It might be a character who doesn't run or take cover while bullets rain down around him. And like others have said, the AI is seemingly without any sense of self-preservation. Even when hopelessly outgunned, bandits and other humans will not flee with their lives; they often choose to stay and fight no matter how many of their allies go down. That being said, the stray dogs have a greater sense of self-preservation - often able to be scared off with a few shots in the air - but this is just not so with the humans. My other major gripe with this game is that your weapons and armor are in a state of constant wear and degradation with no way to repair them. The armor I can understand to a degree - you're replacing your armor usually at a regular pace, and in real life damage to armor plates is usually irreparable except by replacement, but by the time you get the best bits you will want to keep them and sadly that is not an option. However, the lack of the ability to clean or repair your firearms simply astounds me, as gun cleaning you would think would be a basic survival skill in the zone. Not true; guns will start to jam frequently, even after relatively minor degradation, with no way to fix this problem except to sell them and buy a new one, usually at an astronomical cost. The other option is to wait to see if you can find a new one, but this is a dicey proposition, especially in the Zone. I have a few complaints about this game's difficulty, but they are minor. On normal difficulty, a major military force assaults your camp almost right away, on either the first or second day. After a succession of tries I finally survived, but virtually everyone else died. This game employs a hell of a learning curve, and it is not for the faint of heart in the difficulty arena. You must be willing to grind a lot of things out through multiple tries - and unlike other RPG titles, there's no "I'm not at a high enough level" excuse. You either need better equipment or you need to try a new strategy - or you need more experience. That being said, this game has many, many more high points that far outweigh this game's high learning curve. Its atmosphere is almost too perfect; as other reviewers have mentioned its environments are strange, frightening, and sometimes disturbing. STALKER takes full advantage of its decayed Exclusion Zone environment; it has a weirdly immersive environment, with superior graphics and sound that leave you less frightened and more unsettled. The underground sequences are some of the most frightening in the game, and they are just as terrifying for the creatures you will find there as for their poorly lighted, post-military environment. The combat system is enormously entertaining, totally immersive, and is pulse poundingly fast paced and dangerous. For me, some of the game's most difficult moments came when I was forced to finish off downed, whimpering opponents, particularly soldiers (I am a Ukrainian expat and have relatives in the army there). If you are one for nightmares, or have a particular sensitivity towards this sort of thing, this is not a game for you. A minor point but nonetheless important, the weight and carry system is impeccably good, as the enormous weight of large amounts of ammunition is accurately simulated. In many ways, this game reminds me of the tangentially related Arx Fatalis, in which a hero with no memory awakes and has to find his way around a dark world. Though the games have many technical and contextual differences, they are similar enough to make me think the developers of STALKER must have played it. In spite of its flaws this game is just too much fun and is just too good to skip, especially for people who like games like Oblivion and Fallout 3.… Expand

The gameplay makes STALKER shine; with challenging, unforgiving, yet supremely satisfying gunplay, the RPG elements littered about include weapon scavenging, quests and random encounters.
